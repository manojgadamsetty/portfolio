About Me

Hi, I'm Manoj Gadamsetty. I'm a staff software engineer with a passion for building secure, scalable systems that impact millions of users. With over 10 years of specialized experience, I've dedicated my career to mastering mobile development, enterprise security, and identity platforms.

My Journey

I started my career at Flipkart/Walmart, building consumer-facing iOS applications that served millions of users. The experience of shipping high-impact features, optimizing performance, and achieving 99.99% crash-free stability taught me the importance of quality, attention to detail, and user-centric design. From there, I ventured into the enterprise space, working at VMware on mobile device management solutions.

My passion for security and identity platforms drew me to CyberArk, where I spent two years architecting privilege access management systems with enterprise-grade security. This experience shaped my understanding of how critical access control and security are in enterprise environments.

Today, I'm at Palo Alto Networks, leading the development of enterprise identity platforms that power next-generation authentication and zero-trust architecture. I work on implementing OAuth 2.0, OIDC, SAML 2.0, FIDO2, and building secure mobile applications that enterprises trust with their most sensitive operations.

What I'm Passionate About

Security Excellence: I believe security shouldn't be an afterthought. I'm committed to building systems where security is fundamental to the design, not bolted on later. This includes threat modeling, security code reviews, and creating cultures where security is everyone's responsibility.

Performance & Scale: Building systems that serve millions of users reliably and efficiently fascinates me. I love optimizing performance, reducing memory footprints, and architecting systems that scale without compromising stability.

Architecture & Design: I'm deeply interested in system design and architecture—how to build modular, maintainable systems that scale. Whether it's designing APIs, databases, or microservices, I enjoy thinking about trade-offs and making informed architectural decisions.

People & Mentorship: I believe great technology is built by great teams. I've mentored 15+ junior engineers and enjoy helping them grow technically and professionally. I'm passionate about building inclusive, collaborative environments where everyone can do their best work.

Technical Philosophy

I believe in pragmatic engineering—using the right tool for the job, not the fanciest one. I value simplicity, clarity, and maintainability. I'm an advocate for:

  • Security-first thinking in all designs
  • Clear, well-tested code over clever code
  • Data-driven decision making
  • Continuous learning and experimentation
  • Collaboration and knowledge sharing
  • Performance and user experience obsession
